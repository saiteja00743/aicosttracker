# Unit Economics & Revenue Model

## Problem Space Economics
Companies are currently allocating anywhere from $50 to $200 per engineer per month on AI tools (e.g., Copilot + ChatGPT + Midjourney). For a 100-person engineering team, this is $5,000 to $20,000/mo ($60k-$240k/yr). 

Our initial data shows an average **waste rate of 34%** due to:
1. Abandoned seats (people who tried Claude once but stayed subscribed).
2. Overlapping tools (paying for both Copilot and Cursor).
3. Incorrect tiering (paying for Enterprise when Pro suffices).

## Revenue Streams

### 1. The Concierge Negotiation Model (Primary Revenue)
For enterprise clients with $10k+ in identified savings, we operate on a success fee model.
- **Cost to Client:** $0 upfront.
- **Credex Fee:** We take 15% of the first year's realized savings. 
- **LTV:** If we save a company $50,000, our take is $7,500. This is highly compelling for a CFO because it's completely risk-free.

### 2. Startup Infrastructure Credits Partnership (Affiliate/Rev-Share)
We act as an intelligent router for cloud compute and API credits. 
- By partnering with cloud providers (AWS, GCP, Azure), we receive a bounty or rev-share for successfully migrating a startup's API workloads to their ecosystem using startup credits.

### 3. Continuous Monitoring (SaaS Model)
Once a company has been "audited" and optimized, they inevitably accrue waste again as they hire.
- **Pricing:** $199/month for active directory integration that automatically detects inactive AI licenses and downgrades them.
- **CAC to LTV Ratio:** Because the initial audit is a highly viral free tool (PLG), our Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) is near zero ($<50). With a SaaS ARPU of $2,400/yr, our LTV:CAC ratio is phenomenally high.

## Path to $1M ARR
To hit $1M ARR via the SaaS model, we need roughly ~415 active companies paying $199/mo. 
Given the explosive growth of AI tool adoption and the resulting fragmentation, capturing 415 mid-market tech startups is highly achievable within 12-18 months.
